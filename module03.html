How the Product Skill Works
You upload two images. Image 1 is your reference — the aesthetic, the surface, the lighting, the mood you want. Image 2 is your product — the actual jewellery piece from your supplier site or product page. Type the word product. Claude reads both images, extracts everything from the reference, describes your piece accurately, and outputs a complete Higgsfield prompt. You copy it and paste it straight in.
The Golden Rule
Your reference image is everything.

The output will only ever be as good as the reference you give it. A strong reference produces a strong output. A weak reference produces a weak output. Choose your reference carefully.

What makes a strong reference: the lighting must be clear and real. The surface must be physical and believable. The mood must feel like a real photograph. If you look at your reference and it feels real and photographic the output will too.

When the Environment Does Not Look Real
If the output looks CGI or digitally generated the reference image is the problem. It may be too heavily edited, too filtered, or too stylised. Go back and find a reference that feels genuinely photographic. The more real your reference the more real your output.
